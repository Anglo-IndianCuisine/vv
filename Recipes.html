Flavours of the past

Railway Mutton Curry




Railway Mutton Curry was developed during the British Raj by the Indian Railways chefs keeping in mind the British people 's delicate palates. It was first introduced during the Pre-Independence period on the Frontier Mail (Golden Temple Express) operated by the Western Railway. This milder version of the classic mutton curry wasn't too spicy yet an excellent fusion dish that blended the taste of Indian and English spices . ...More

Mince Ball Curry




Meatball curry is an classic Anglo Indian food recipe. Meatballs aka koftas, are minced meat balls flavored with green chilies and coriander, that are slow cooked in a spiced gravy. The meatballs are tender, moist, and flavorful in a perfectly spiced tomato based curry. The meatball curry is usually served with aromatic coconut rice ...More

Bread And Butter Pudding




Bread and butter pudding is a traditional type of pudding that is popular in the Anglo-Indian cuisine. It is made by layering slices of buttered bread scattered with nuts or raisins in an oven dish, and is seasoned with an egg custard mixture or caramel, made with milk or cream ...More

The Anglo-Indian Cutlets, is a popular Anglo-Indian dish that never fails to impress by its outer crispy texture. In this dish lamb or chicken mince and potato cutlets, combine together to give an excellent outer crispiness with a softer texture on the inside. Shepherd's Pie




Shepherd's pie or cottage pie is a minced meat pie with a crust or topping of mashed potato and cheese. The recipe has many variations, but the main ingredients are minced meat with onions and a layer of mashed potato and cheese on top ...More

Soft tangzhong Bread




Tangzhong is a yeast bread technique. It involves cooking a portion of the flour and liquid in the recipe into a thick slur prior to adding the remaining ingredients, resulting in soft, fluffy bread. This pre-cooking accomplishes two positive things: it makes bread or rolls softer and more tender, and extends their shelf life. ...More
